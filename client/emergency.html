Emergency Safety Guides

Flood Safety Guide
Before Emergency
  • Keep emergency supplies, radio, and flashlights ready.
  • Identify local shelter routes and elevated zones.
  • Store essential documents in waterproof bags.
During Emergency
  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • Do not walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters.
  • Disconnect appliances if safe to do so.
After Emergency
  • Avoid standing water due to contamination or power risk.
  • Drink only clean or boiled water.
  • Inspect buildings carefully before re-entering.
Building Fire Guide
Before Emergency
  • Verify smoke detectors and fire extinguisher locations.
  • Identify at least two escape exits from every room.
During Emergency
  • Crawl low under smoke to exit safely.
  • Touch doors before opening—do not open if hot.
  • If clothes catch fire: Stop, Drop, and Roll.
After Emergency
  • Call 101 from outside the building.
  • Do not re-enter a burning building.
Earthquake Safety Guide
Before Emergency
  • Secure heavy furniture to wall studs.
  • Prepare emergency food and water kits.
During Emergency
  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On under a desk or table.
  • Stay away from glass and exterior walls.
  • If outdoors, stay clear of power lines and structures.
After Emergency
  • Expect aftershocks. Check for injuries.
  • Inspect utility lines before using switches.
Cyclone & Storm Guide
Before Emergency
  • Board up windows and secure loose outdoor items.
  • Keep phones and power banks charged.
During Emergency
  • Stay indoors in an interior room away from windows.
  • Remain inside during the eye of the storm.
After Emergency
  • Beware of fallen electric wires and branches.
  • Wait for official clearance before exiting shelter.
Road Accident & Medical Guide
Before Emergency
  • Keep vehicle first-aid kits and emergency contacts ready.
During Emergency
  • Call 108 or 112 immediately.
  • Turn on hazard lights to protect the accident scene.
  • Do not move injured persons unless fire risk exists.
After Emergency
  • Provide clear landmarks to emergency dispatchers.
